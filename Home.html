Prelude

The girl was always upset about the noise from the apartment upstairs, but never made the flight upstairs to complain about it. Little did she know, that she would fall for the troublemaker.

 

Vivek’s version

If ever, me or my roommate pointed out that the TV was playing too loud to disturb the neighbours, we would laugh it off, that the volume is only going to disturb the girls downstairs, and this might be a good opportunity to meet them. Never realized this minor offense would label me as troublemaker by HER for the rest of my life.

 

My friend Dharav used to come to my place every now and then. Couple of times he did mention that the girl downstairs works with him, but he never took the trouble of introducing us. When he got married to Hiral, I decided to cook lunch for them (a rare initiative) and she insisted on meeting Eshita the same evening. First time I saw her smile, her laugh, I knew that I have to make her smile everyday.

 

Eshita’s version

The first time Vivek came to pick up a wrongly delivered package of his, I looked the other way. When destiny is a determined Punjabi guy, there is no escaping it. Even if you ignore it, however noisy, it will seek its way.  

 

An impromptu invite by Dharav and Hiral landed me at his door. Little did I know,  the noisy guy with his melodious talks will hit the right cords. He seemed to be very sweet, charming, friendly and a fun loving guy. Somewhere I knew there was a spark between us and the glint in his eyes, just stayed with me as I left his apartment.

 

Since we met, we have shared some magical moments, have laughed together, I cried (Vivek forced me into writing this :P), and each of these moments has made us love each other even more.  

 

Prologue

Marriages are made in heaven, so are thunder and lightning. Dharav knew that their meeting will be like lightning, so he tried to keep them apart, but then destiny finds its way. So we invite you to bear this thunderstorm which strikes on 19th April 2015.
